Shoplifting on the rise

Shoplifting has been on the rise costing retailers billions of dollars of lost revenue. Based on the provided data, retailers are expected to lose over $55 Billions in 2028.

Understanding preventative measures against shoplifting is important. Here are a few of many methods that have been put in place to combat shoplifting across retailers.

Price Adjustments

About 64% of retail buisnesses implement this method of approach. It serves as a deterrent, but it does not prevent the theft from happening.

Surveillance and Motion Technology

About 45% of retail buinsesses use motion sensors. Motion sensors are meant to capture any movement that maybe security cameras may not have seen.

Employee Vetting

About 43% of retail businesses do background checks on employees before hiring them. This helps reduce stealing from the inside.

Increased Staffing

About 36% of retail businesses hire more staff to monitor customers. It is a good method too, not just having them observe, but being able to actively monitor everyone in the stores will help reduce theft because people will be more afriad to steal with the increased presences of employees.

Combating Retail Theft

Proactive prevention strategies, from technology to staffing, are important in minimizing retail theft. These strategies will play a role in the rational choice of people and hopefully stop them from committing retail theft.
